<p><p>Functionalized Polyethylene Glycols (PEGs) are a type of polymer that are widely used in various industries. The market for PEGs can be classified into different types based on their structure and functionality.</p><p>Linear PEGs have a straightforward structure, with a single chain of PEG molecules. They are commonly used in drug delivery, tissue engineering, and surface modification applications.</p><p>Branched PEGs have multiple branches stemming from a central core, enabling greater control over their properties. They find applications in drug delivery systems and biomedical research.</p><p>Multi-Arm PEGs consist of several arms stemming from a central core, offering enhanced stability and functionality. They are used in various fields including drug delivery, diagnostics, and bioconjugation for targeted therapy.</p><p>Other markets for PEGs include functionalized PEG derivatives, PEGylated proteins, and PEGylated liposomes. These derivatives have specific functional groups added to the PEG structure, providing unique properties for different applications such as drug delivery, imaging, and diagnostics.</p></p>
